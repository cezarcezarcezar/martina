About Martina

Light as a Medium

Martina is a passionate student artist with an eye for the human form and the stories etched into every face she encounters.

By day, she fills her sketchbooks and canvases with expressive oil and watercolor portraits, capturing the subtle interplay of light and shadow across her subjects' features with remarkable sensitivity. By night, she wanders her campus and city streets with her camera in hand, documenting candid moments — a laughing stranger, a contemplative elder, a child lost in play — always searching for the raw, unguarded emotion that a single photograph can freeze in time. Her studio walls are a testament to her dual passions: bold, textured paintings hang beside crisp black-and-white prints, each piece a different lens through which she explores what it means to be human. For Martina, whether the medium is a brush or a shutter, every portrait is an act of connection — a quiet conversation between artist and subject that transcends words.

"Colour is my day-long obsession, joy and torment."
